Glossary
// understanding baseball statistics in context
Offensive Statistics
AVG (Batting Average)
Hits divided by at-bats. Measures a batter's ability to make contact and get hits. League average is around .270.
OBP (On-Base Percentage)
(Hits + Walks + Hit-by-pitch) / (At-bats + Walks + Hit-by-pitch + Sacrifice flies). Measures how often a player reaches base. League average around .330.
SLG (Slugging Percentage)
Total bases / at-bats. Measures power hitting (1B=1, 2B=2, 3B=3, HR=4). League average around .410.
xwOBA (Expected Weighted On-Base Average)
A Statcast metric that predicts a batter's actual average based on exit velocity and launch angle. More predictive than actual results.
K% (Strikeout Rate)
Strikeouts divided by plate appearances. Higher is worse for batters. League average around 22%.
BB% (Walk Rate)
Walks divided by plate appearances. Higher is better as it reaches base and doesn't make an out. League average around 8%.
Whiff%
Percentage of pitches swung at and missed. Shows susceptibility to poor pitches and swing-and-miss stuff.
HR (Home Runs)
Number of four-base hits. Most valuable offensive output. Top hitters hit 40+ per season; league average batter ~20.
Pitching Statistics
ERA (Earned Run Average)
Earned runs allowed × 9 / innings pitched. Lower is better. Elite pitchers have ERAs under 3.00; league average ~4.00.
FIP (Fielding Independent Pitching)
Estimates ERA based only on strikeouts, walks, HRs (not hits or fielding). More predictive than ERA. Lower is better.
WHIP (Walks + Hits per Innings Pitched)
(Walks + Hits) / Innings Pitched. Shows efficiency. Lower is better. Elite under 1.10; league average ~1.30.
K/9 (Strikeouts per 9 innings)
Strikeout rate normalized to 9-inning game. Shows dominant strikeout stuff. Elite pitchers have K/9 over 10; average ~9.
BB/9 (Walks per 9 innings)
Walk rate normalized to 9-inning game. Lower is better (more control). Elite under 2.0; average ~3.5.
HR/9 (Home Runs per 9 innings)
Home run rate normalized to 9-inning game. Lower is better. Elite under 0.8; average ~1.0.
Advanced Concepts
Count State (Balls-Strikes)
Pitcher-friendly counts (0-0, 0-1, 0-2, 1-2) favor strikeouts. Batter-friendly counts (2-0, 3-0, 3-1) favor hits and walks. 3-2 is the ultimate hitter's count with high walk rates.
Pitch Sequencing
The strategy of throwing pitches in a specific order to set up hitters. Early strike calls increase later whiff rates. Fastballs after off-speed often miss further.
Pitch Mix
Distribution of pitch types thrown. Common types: Fastball (FF), Slider (SL), Curveball (CU), Changeup (CH), Sinker (SI). Better pitchers can vary mix by count.
Statcast Data
Pitch-by-pitch tracking from every MLB game. Includes exit velocity, launch angle, spin rate, and location. Enables predictive metrics like xwOBA.
